# Design

The design of UXistentialism Studio expresses a single belief: that thinking
deserves an environment built for depth rather than speed. Every design
decision is measured against whether it helps an idea mature.

## Design Principles

1. **Maturation over production.**
   The interface never rewards volume or velocity. It makes progress legible in
   terms of depth — how considered an idea is — not how much was produced.

2. **The Idea is the subject.**
   The user works on ideas, not files. Documents and artifacts appear as
   outputs of an idea, never as the thing itself. The design keeps the idea and
   its development at the center.

3. **Spaces are modes, not folders.**
   Each of the six spaces puts the user in a distinct posture of thinking and a
   distinct relationship to time. The design of a space should make its mode
   felt: Today orients in the present, Field opens to the ongoing world,
   Formation focuses on what is emerging, Iteration reworks the near past,
   Distribution composes for release, Memory reflects on the accumulated past.

4. **Connections are part of the material.**
   Relationships between ideas, observations, sources, and discussions are
   first-class, so the design treats them as content to be authored and read —
   not as an afterthought or a separate "graph view." A graph is one way to see
   the connections; it is not the only place they live.

5. **Continuity is the core feature.**
   Nothing is lost. Work carries its history with it as it moves between modes.
   The design surfaces lineage — where an idea came from and what it became — so
   the body of work feels cumulative, not disposable.

6. **Calm, not urgent.**
   No streaks, no red badges, no pressure to keep up. The tone is measured and
   unhurried. Attention is invited, never demanded.

7. **The tool recedes.**
   Chrome, controls, and configuration stay quiet. The interface earns its
   presence only when it helps the thinking, then gets out of the way.

## Experience Qualities

The Studio should feel **considered, continuous, and quiet**. Considered — every
surface reflects care. Continuous — the work has memory, lineage, and
connection. Quiet — the environment holds attention without competing for it.

## Visual Direction

The visual system is intentionally deferred until the core lifecycle is
proven in interaction. When it is defined, it will follow from these principles
rather than precede them. Initial direction: generous space, restrained color,
typography treated as a first-class material, and motion used only to express
continuity and connection between ideas and modes.

_This document defines intent. A concrete visual language, component library,
and tokens will be specified once the core experience is validated._
